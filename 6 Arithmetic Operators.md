# Heading: Arithmetic Operators in Python

### Syntax: a <operator> b

# üîç Breakdown
# Used to perform mathematical operations on numbers.

# üíª Common Arithmetic Operators
# | Operator | Name                | Example   | Output |
# | -------- | ------------------- | --------- | ------ |
# | +        | Addition            | 10 + 5    | 15     |
# | -        | Subtraction         | 10 - 5    | 5      |
# | *        | Multiplication      | 10 * 5    | 50     |
# | /        | Division            | 10 / 5    | 2.0    |
# | //       | Floor Division      | 10 // 3   | 3      |
# | %        | Modulus (Remainder) | 10 % 3    | 1      |
# | **       | Exponent (Power)    | 2 ** 3    | 8      |

# üíª Examples
a = 10
b = 3
print(a + b)   # Output: 13
print(a // b)  # Output: 3
print(a ** b)  # Output: 1000

# üí° Tip
# Division (/) always gives a float, even if the result divides evenly.

# ‚úÖ In Short
# Arithmetic operators allow you to perform basic math operations on numbers in Python.
